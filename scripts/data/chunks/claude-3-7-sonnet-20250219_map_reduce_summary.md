# Caring for Thyroid Health

This presentation discusses the importance of the thyroid as the body's natural adaptogen system and how to maintain thyroid health. The thyroid produces hormones that help humans adapt to changing environmental conditions, regulate metabolism, and support various bodily functions. Throughout human evolution, the thyroid has been crucial for survival, helping our ancestors adapt to temperature changes, food scarcity, and other environmental challenges.

The speaker explains that thyroid problems, particularly hypothyroidism, are common today, especially among women. Hypothyroidism symptoms include brain fog, fatigue, decreased cognitive function, metabolic issues, weight gain, water retention, elevated cholesterol, insulin resistance, and joint pain. These symptoms reflect the thyroid's crucial role in regulating metabolism, supporting immune function, and facilitating tissue regeneration.

To maintain thyroid health, the speaker recommends first getting comprehensive testing, including blood work and ultrasound, before making any changes. Key nutrients for thyroid health include iodine, selenium, zinc, iron, copper, vitamin D3, vitamin A (retinol), B vitamins, and vitamin C. The speaker suggests a balanced diet with moderate calories, adequate protein and fats, and fewer carbohydrates. Coconut oil is specifically mentioned as potentially beneficial for thyroid function.

## Techniques and Approaches for Thyroid Health

- **Testing and Monitoring**: Complete blood work (including TSH, FT3, FT4, anti-TPO), thyroid ultrasound, and regular monitoring
- **Nutrient Supplementation**: Balanced intake of iodine (1-3mg daily), selenium (up to 100mcg), zinc, iron, copper, vitamins A, D3, B, and C
- **Dietary Approach**: Moderate calories, balanced macronutrients with emphasis on protein and fats, lower carbohydrates
- **Elimination Diet**: Removing potential inflammatory foods like gluten and dairy
- **Herbal Support**: Ashwagandha and Coleus forskohlii to stimulate thyroid function
- **Reducing Exposure**: Limiting halogen exposure (fluoride, chlorine, bromine) and goitrogens

## Detailed Points

- The thyroid is our body's internal adaptogen system that helps us adjust to environmental changes
- Thyroid hormones were crucial for human survival throughout evolution
- Thyroid health affects metabolism, temperature regulation, cognitive function, immune response, and tissue healing
- Hypothyroidism is more common than hyperthyroidism, especially among women
- Factors that damage thyroid function include:
  - Radioactive iodine exposure
  - Excess halogens (fluoride, chlorine, bromine)
  - Goitrogenic substances in foods like cruciferous vegetables and soy
  - Chronic inflammation
  - Prolonged caloric restriction
  - Autoimmune conditions
- Iodine is essential for thyroid hormone production but must be balanced with other nutrients
- Selenium protects the thyroid during hormone production and helps convert T4 to T3
- Iron is needed for thyroid peroxidase enzyme function
- Zinc is required for thyroid hormone receptors to function properly
- Copper helps with iron utilization
- Vitamin D3 (optimal level 80 ng/ml) protects against autoimmune thyroid conditions
- Vitamin A as retinol supports the sodium-iodide symporter and regulates immune function
- B vitamins provide energy for thyroid processes
- Vitamin C improves iodine transport to the thyroid
- Coconut oil may improve thyroid function according to some practitioners
- Omega-3 fatty acids help reduce inflammation
- Ashwagandha and Coleus forskohlii can stimulate thyroid function
- Before supplementing, comprehensive testing is essential to determine thyroid health status
